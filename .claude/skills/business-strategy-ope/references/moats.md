# The 15 Competitive Moats

Reference for moat analysis and selection. Present these to users in context of their specific business.

## Group 1: Product & Value Moats

### 1. Differentiation Moat
**What it is:** Unique features or solutions that stand out from competitors.
**Example:** Slack differentiated with user-friendly interface and countless integrations.
**Best for:** Companies with genuinely unique IP, approach, or capabilities.
**Questions to assess:**
- What do you do that competitors literally cannot copy?
- What makes customers say "I've never seen this before"?

### 2. Cost Leadership Moat
**What it is:** Delivering value at lowest cost while maintaining quality.
**Example:** Zoom offered superior reliability at lower price than competitors.
**Best for:** Companies with operational efficiencies, economies of scale.
**Questions to assess:**
- Can you sustainably offer lower prices than competitors?
- What structural advantages enable your cost position?

### 3. User Experience Moat
**What it is:** Exceptional, intuitive experience that keeps users engaged.
**Example:** Notion gained traction through clean, flexible workspace design.
**Best for:** Consumer products, tools competing in crowded markets.
**Questions to assess:**
- Do users describe your product as "delightful" or "intuitive"?
- Is UX a genuine differentiator or table stakes in your market?

### 4. Pricing Moat
**What it is:** Flexible pricing models aligned with customer needs/budgets.
**Example:** Canva offers generous free plan + $12.99 Pro vs Adobe's $54.99.
**Best for:** Freemium products, PLG companies, price-sensitive markets.
**Questions to assess:**
- Does your pricing model create structural advantage?
- Can competitors easily match your pricing approach?

### 5. Free Moat
**What it is:** Reducing barriers via free version to attract and acquire users.
**Example:** ChatGPT attracted 1M users in 5 days with free access.
**Best for:** Products with viral potential, network effects, or upsell paths.
**Questions to assess:**
- Can you sustain a free tier economically?
- Does free usage create value (data, network, brand)?

## Group 2: Stickiness & Growth Moats

### 6. Network Effects Moat
**What it is:** Product value increases as more users join the ecosystem.
**Example:** LinkedIn thrives with 950M+ members connecting professionals.
**Best for:** Social platforms, collaboration tools, marketplaces.
**Questions to assess:**
- Does each new user make the product more valuable for others?
- What's your network density threshold for value?

### 7. Switching Cost Moat
**What it is:** Making it costly/challenging to switch to competitors.
**Example:** Tableau locks in users with deep integrations, custom dashboards, training.
**Best for:** Enterprise products, data platforms, workflow tools.
**Questions to assess:**
- What would a customer lose by leaving?
- How deep are your integrations into customer workflows?

### 8. Engagement Moat
**What it is:** Building stickiness through frequent interaction and reliance.
**Example:** GitHub created engagement via pull requests, issue tracking, social coding.
**Best for:** Developer tools, communities, products with high usage frequency.
**Questions to assess:**
- How often do users interact with your product?
- Do users become advocates and evangelists?

### 9. Expansion Moat
**What it is:** Increasing LTV by making it easy to expand usage or add features.
**Example:** HubSpot started as free CRM, expanded to Marketing and Sales Hub.
**Best for:** Platform products, products with multiple use cases.
**Questions to assess:**
- What's your natural expansion path?
- Do customers organically discover more value over time?

### 10. Proprietary Moat
**What it is:** Protection through patents, unique tech, or exclusive data.
**Example:** NVIDIA leads GPU market with 15,000+ patents in AI computing.
**Best for:** Deep tech, companies with significant R&D investment.
**Questions to assess:**
- What IP do you own that others cannot replicate?
- How defensible is your technology long-term?

## Group 3: Market & Brand Moats

### 11. Operational Efficiency Moat
**What it is:** Superior efficiency through optimized processes and scale.
**Example:** AWS leverages massive economies of scale for competitive cloud pricing.
**Best for:** Infrastructure, logistics, high-volume operations.
**Questions to assess:**
- Do you have structural operational advantages?
- Can you achieve economies of scale competitors cannot?

### 12. Branding and Reputation Moat
**What it is:** Trust and loyalty through strong, recognizable brand.
**Example:** Stripe built trusted brand via developer-friendly approach and reliability.
**Best for:** Products where trust matters, B2B with long sales cycles.
**Questions to assess:**
- Would customers pay premium for your brand?
- Do customers choose you based on reputation alone?

### 13. Distribution Moat
**What it is:** Access to unique or extensive distribution channels.
**Example:** Clay grew to 100K+ users through strategic partnerships (Anthropic, Notion, Vanta).
**Best for:** Products with partnership potential, B2B with channel opportunities.
**Questions to assess:**
- What distribution channels do you own or have exclusive access to?
- Can you reach customers in ways competitors cannot?

### 14. Speed and Innovation Moat
**What it is:** Rapid product updates and innovations to stay ahead.
**Example:** Figma frequently introduces real-time collaboration features.
**Best for:** Fast-moving markets, products competing on features.
**Questions to assess:**
- Can you ship faster than competitors?
- Is innovation velocity a sustainable advantage?

### 15. Founder Brand Moat
**What it is:** Using founder's reputation to create trust and visibility.
**Example:** Superhuman leveraged Rahul Vohra's brand for early adopters.
**Best for:** Early-stage companies, products in crowded markets.
**Questions to assess:**
- Does the founder have significant industry credibility?
- Can founder visibility be converted to customer trust?

---

## Moat Selection Framework

When helping users select 3 moats:

1. **Eliminate non-starters**: Which moats are structurally impossible for their business?
2. **Identify natural fits**: Which moats align with existing strengths?
3. **Consider timeline**: Which moats can be built vs. already exist?
4. **Assess sustainability**: Which moats are defensible long-term?

**Red flags:**
- Selecting moats they don't actually have
- Choosing moats that conflict (cost leadership + premium branding)
- Picking moats competitors already own in their market
